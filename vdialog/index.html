Task Defination

VDialogUE is a multi-task multi-domain visually-grounded dialog benchmark with the goal of providing an accessible platform and a standard practice for the evaluation of general vision-dialogue model. As shown in below, VDialogUE consists of six different datasets spanning five different tasks: Multi-Modal Intent Prediction, Multi-Modal Dialog Retrieval (T2I), Multi-Modal Dialog Retrieval (I2T), Multi-Modal Dialog State Tracking and Multi-Modal Response Generation, where T2I and I2T are short for text-to-image and image-to-text, respectively.

Abstract

Visually-grounded dialog systems, which integrate multiple modes of communication such as text and visual inputs, have attracted increasing attention from the field of natural language processing (NLP). While utilizing visual modality has the potential to enhance the richness and flexibility of dialog systems, the lack of a unified evaluation framework makes it difficult to compare and evaluate various approaches to different multi-modal dialog tasks. To this end, we propose VDialogUE, a Visually-grounded Dialogue benchmark for Unified Evaluation. To encourage the adoption of VDialogUE in the multi-modal dialog community, we develop an open-source evaluation script and a leaderboard that allows researchers to easily compare the performance of their visually-grounded dialog systems. In addition, we provide a simple yet effective baseline model called VISIT (VISually-grounded dIalog Transformer), which is a multi-modal conversation model pre-trained on both large-scale non-dialog image-text pairs and our limited visually-grounded dialog corpora VDialogUE. We believe that the VDialogUE benchmark, along with the evaluation scripts and our baseline models, will accelerate the development of visually-grounded dialog systems and lead to the development of more sophisticated and effective pre-trained models.
